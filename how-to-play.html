玩法说明

黄金之路 LoL 是一款由玩家制作的英雄联盟电竞阵容选秀策略游戏。你将依次通过转盘随机生成历史战队与年份，为未锁定的位置选择一名选手，并尝试组建一套强力首发阵容，在赛季模拟中赢下每一个赛事，挑战完美的“黄金之路”。

1. 什么是黄金之路？

在《英雄联盟》电竞历史中，“黄金之路”（Golden Road）代表着一支队伍在单个赛季中包揽所有的主要冠军头衔。这包括赛区内部联赛冠军（春季赛/第一赛段、夏季赛/第二赛段、第三赛段等）以及国际大赛（季中冠军赛 MSI、全球总决赛 Worlds 等）。

在本游戏的黄金之路主模式中，你的阵容需要面临春季赛（第一赛段）、First Stand 国际赛、夏季赛（第二赛段）、MSI（季中冠军赛）、第三赛段和全球总决赛的完整考验。只有全部斩获第一名，才算完成黄金之路挑战。而在 MSI 挑战模式中，你将使用精选的 MSI 历史阵容，进行入围赛、小组赛、淘汰赛和决赛的精简模拟。

2. 转盘选秀机制

每一轮选秀都将转动 赛区、战队、年份 三个轮盘：

  • 轮盘决定候选范围（例如抽到 2019 年的 G2 战队），展示该战队当年的首发大名单。
  • 你无需拘泥于选手的现实位置。只要你的阵容中该位置仍然空闲（例如打野位），你就可以将其锁定放入你的首发名单。
  • 重复 5 轮选秀，填满你的上单、打野、中单、ADC、辅助 阵容。

3. 选手限制与位置稀缺性

在一次挑战中，每位现实选手只能被挑选一次。例如，如果你在第一轮选择了 2018 年的 Faker，那么即使后面抽到了 2023 年的 T1，你也无法再次选择 Faker。

优秀的阵容需要平衡核心输出（中单、ADC）与绿叶位置（打野、辅助）的评分。在轮盘随机性极强的情况下，当遇到冷门战队但拥有极高水平的辅助或打野时，优先锁定稀缺位置往往是通关的关键。

4. 重骰机制 (Rerolls)

游戏提供有限的重骰机会以调整不佳的抽取结果：

  • 赛区重骰：更换赛区，尽量保持当前的年份。
  • 战队重骰：在相同赛区和年份中随机更换另一只战队。
  • 年份重骰：保留当前战队与赛区，更换到该俱乐部历史上的其他可用年份。
💡 策略提示：早期的轮次可以接受中规中矩的选手，把重骰留给后两轮。在只剩一两个特定位置空缺时，遭遇位置冲突的概率极高，此时保留的重骰机会将成为你的救命稻草。

5. 阵容评分与赛季模拟

选人结束后，系统会计算你 5 名选手的隐藏历史评分（评分范围为 1-99，与该选手的特定年份表现绑定）的平均值，并加入同战队/同赛区羁绊加成，得出最终的阵容评分。系统将基于此评分和随机概率算法（含有赛事爆冷与势头机制）进行模拟。哪怕你的阵容拿到了 95+ 的超神评分，也不能保证百分之百夺冠，这正是电竞赛事的魅力所在！

How to Play

Golden Road LoL is a fan-made League of Legends esports roster-building strategy game. Spin wheels to randomly select a historical team and season, draft a player for an open role, and try to build a 5-man roster strong enough to win every championship in a simulated season.

1. What is the Golden Road?

In League of Legends esports, the "Golden Road" represents the ultimate achievement of winning every major title available in a competitive season. This includes domestic league splits and international tournaments (MSI and Worlds).

In the main Golden Road mode, your team will compete in Split 1, First Stand, Split 2, MSI, Split 3, and Worlds. To succeed, your roster must finish first at all 6 stops. The MSI Challenge mode focuses on memorable MSI rosters from 2015 to 2026, running a shorter tournament simulation: Play-In, Group Stage, Knockouts, and Final.

2. The Wheel Draft System

Each drafting round spins three wheels: Region, Team, and Year.

  • The wheels stop on a specific team-season (e.g., 2019 G2), showing that year's starting roster.
  • You are not forced to draft players into their real-life positions. As long as a position is open on your fantasy lineup, you can assign any player from the roll to it.
  • Repeat this for 5 rounds to fill all positions: Top, Jungle, Mid, ADC, and Support.

3. Player Limitations & Role Scarcity

Each real player can only be drafted once per run. For example, if you draft 2018 Faker, he becomes unavailable later even if another team-year with Faker appears on the wheels.

Strong drafts balance star carries (Mid/ADC) with crucial "glue" roles (Jungle/Support). Since rolls are random, recognizing a highly-rated Support or Jungler from a lesser-known team-year is often the key to surviving the season.

4. Using Rerolls

You have limited rerolls to save your draft from difficult combinations:

  • Region Reroll: Changes the region, trying to maintain the current year.
  • Team Reroll: Picks another team from the same region and year.
  • Year Reroll: Keeps the team and region, moving to another valid historical year for that organization.
💡 Strategy Tip: Accept decent early drafts and save your rerolls. When you have only one or two open slots left, you are highly susceptible to role blocks, making saved rerolls crucial for survival.

5. Team Rating & Simulation

Once your roster is complete, the game averages the players' hidden historical ratings (1-99, based on their performance in that specific year) and applies synergy bonuses (same team or region familiarity) to generate a final Team Rating. The simulation engine calculates tournament outcomes using opponent strength and momentum logic. Even a 95+ rated superteam can suffer upsets, capturing the true spirit of esports!
